Our Monastery

Magistri Traditionis monastery is located in the heart of Alexandria, Ontario. As the name suggests, our monastery is dedicated to the preservation of Catholic tradition. In doing so, our monastery hosts week-long retreats for catholic priests interested in learning how to celebrate mass from the 1962 Roman Missal - the latest form of the traditional Latin mass.

The Monastery

Housing 33 monks, Magistri Traditionis monastery sits on the surrounding properties of St. Finnan Basilica. The monastery also has facilities for hosting groups of up to 12 priests at a time, metal-working garages, classrooms, and a beautiful garden.

The founders of the monastery intentionally chose to be affiliated with St. Finnan's as the Basilica has three traditional altars (as can be seen in the second image in the slideshow above). Having these three altars was important because it meant that multiple traditional masses could be celebrated at a time. This is necessary when the monks are hosting retreats with a larger group of priests and need the extra altars for teaching them the Latin mass.

The Monks

All monks at Magistri Traditionis are priests ordained in the Priestly Fraternity of Saint Peter (FSSP). This is a requirement as FSSP priests are taught to celebrate the mass “according to the traditional Roman Rite”1.

Daily Life

Prayer

On top of attending daily mass and praying lauds and vespers, monks at Magistri Traditionis must pray all twenty decades of the rosary every day. Starting with the joyful mysteries after lauds, then praying the sorrowful mysteries after lunch, followed by the glorious mysteries after vespers and finishing with the luminous mysteries before bed.

Teaching

Second to prayer, teaching is the next most important part of the monks' daily lives. Teaching at Magistri Traditionis is focused on the instruction of the traditional Latin mass. Such instruction is carried out in three different ways at the monastery:

  1. Workshops: As mentioned, the monastery's primary activity involves hosting retreats for teaching priests to celebrate the extraordinary form of the mass. On such retreats, priests will attend workshops hosted by monks. Common workshops are: Latin Pronunciation, Gregorian Chant and Liturgical Norms.
  2. Writing: Monks are also encouraged to advocate for the celebration of the traditional mass beyond the walls of the monastery. This can be accomplished by writing articles, books and even appearing on podcasts.
  3. Supporting: This type of teaching involves answering letters and phone calls from priests about various intricacies pertaining to the celebration of the traditional mass.
Work

The use of incense is an important ingredient for the beauty and mystery of the mass, and an essential part of the traditional liturgy. Therefore, monks at Magistri Traditionis spend part of their time crafting thuribles - the vessels for holding and burning incense. In the spring and summertime, the production of thuribles slows down as the monks take on the responsibility of tending to the monastery's garden.

Beliefs

Patron Saints



Saint Michael

Guardian of paradise, may Saint Michael defend the Magistri Traditionis monastery from all demons attacks.

Saint Jerome

Author of the Vulgate, Saint Jerome is a patriarch of the Latin language. May he guide our hand in all of our writings.

Saint Joseph

Terror of demons, may Saint Joseph protect the monks of Magistri Traditionis as he protected Jesus and Mary from Herod.

Motto

Imitating the motto of Benedictine orders, “ora et labora”2 is the motto of Magistri Traditionis, meaning pray and work. It is believed that idleness is a sure path to sin, therefore the monks are encouraged to dive deeply into their work and prayer. In addition, St. Paul advises “to keep away from believers who are living in idleness” (2 Thes 3:6 - NRSV). As such, the monks are subject to dense daily working and prayer schedules. Here is a typical day for a monk:

AM PM
  • 7am - lauds, rosary, mass
  • 9am - breakfast
  • 10am - teach Latin workshop
  • 12pm - lunch, rosary
  • 1pm - teach Gregorian chant workshop
  • 3pm - help cook
  • 5pm - dinner
  • 6pm - vespers, rosary
  • 7pm - metal working
  • 10pm - last rosary

Controversies

One of the main controversies at the Magistri Traditionis monastery is the praying of the Luminous mysteries of the rosary as a part of the mandatory daily rosary that needs to be prayed by every monk. This prayer is controversial among the monks as it was instituted by Pope John Paul II less than three decades ago in 2002 3. Given their fierce dedication to tradition, some of the monks at Magistri Traditionis are resistive to praying such a relatively novel prayer in relation to the history of the Church. In fact, since the Luminous mysteries are scheduled to be prayed privately before bed, it is rumoured that some of the monks rebel by praying the Glorious mysteries instead.

Rule

Technology

Monks are forbidden from owning any personal digital device such as a smartphone or laptop. If monks require a device for work related matters they may use one of the computers in the library. Monks are also allowed to use the computers for spiritual research and writing. Monks are allowed to own MP3 players and use them for listening to music, but they must use headphones when doing so.

Mealtimes

Monks are to take turns cooking, serving, and doing dishes according to a schedule. In addition, as inspired by chapter 38 in the rule of St. Benedict, there is to be a reader during mealtimes who will read spiritual texts the monks. During such times, there must be no talking among the monks, even for practical reasons “let the Brethren so minister to each other, that no one need ask for anything”4.

Recreation

Monks may only leave the monastery upon being granted permission by an elder. In general, monks should not leave the monastery more than once a month. Monks may have possessions that enable their recreation and hobbies, so long as they are not digital. For example, musical instruments, painting supplies, radios and carving supplies are allowed on site. Monks may smoke cigars and pipes indoors, so long as they are mindful of their ashes. Cigarettes must be smoked outdoors. Drinking is permitted.

Penance

The penitential at Magistri Tranditionis monastery is based on the seven deadly sins. As per paragraph 1434 in the Catechism of the Catholic Church, prayer, fasting, and almsgiving are considered to be the highest expressions of penance 5. Thus, Magistri Tranditionis prioritizes such exercises for penance. Lastly, the following contains only a few examples of typical penances and is not an exhaustive list.

Pride

  • If a monk irreverently protests orders from a superior, he is to fast from bread for a day.
  • If a superior speaks rudely to a monk, he is to fast from bread for three days.
  • If a monk gets into a vain argument, he is to fast from meat for three days.

Anger

  • If a monk curses, he is to say a decade of the Rosary.
  • If a monk breaks anything in a fit of rage, he is to fix or replace it and pray the Sorrowful Mysteries of the Rosary.
  • If a monk raises his voice in an argument, he is to give $10 to the Church.

Lust

  • If a monk hugs a woman for more than 5 seconds, he is to say three Hail Mary's plus one for every second he hugged her beyond the first 5.
  • If a monk kisses a woman on the lips, he is to pray a decade of the Rosary.
  • If a monk calls a woman an affectionate name, he is to pray a decade of the Rosary.

Envy

  • If a monk gossips, he is to pray the Sorrowful mysteries of the Rosary.
  • If a monk insults another's work, he is to give $30 to the Church.
  • If a monk lies about another, he is to only have one meal the next day.

Gluttony

  • If a monk breaks his Lenten fast, he is to abstain from bread for a day.
  • If a monk becomes drunk, he is to abstain from beer for a month.
  • If a monk smokes weed, he is to give $20 to the Church.

Avarice

  • If a monk strikes another, he is to fast from meat for two weeks.
  • If a monk steals, he is to return what he stole and give $50 to the Church.
  • If a monk protests his pay, he is to pray a decade of the Rosary.

Sloth

  • If a monk oversleeps, he is to pray a Hail Mary for every minute he overslept.
  • If a monk falls asleep in mass, he is to pray the Sorrowful mysteries of the Rosary.
  • If a monk diminishes Church teaching, he is to fast from meat for a week.
